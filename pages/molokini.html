Site Overview

Molokini Crater, Maui

The southeastern rim of a submerged volcanic crater that creates a protected interior with ocean-blue visibility. Our private charters arrive before the crowd and hold the dive sequence to the calmer side of the basin.

Molokini is not a broad reef; it is a crescent of volcanic wall and sand channels. The interior reef grows in shallow terraces, while the west-facing wall drops into deep blue water. This combination makes it one of the clearest dive sites in Hawaii.

What you will see

Pyramid butterflyfish and kahala along the wall.
Blacktip reef sharks and spotted eagle rays in the basin.
Widespread hard coral gardens and octocoral colonies.
Long-range visibility, often exceeding 80–100 feet.

Why this charter

Private boats keep a small guest count and early departure from Maalaea Harbor, reducing drift and offering a quiet return to the harbor well before afternoon southerly winds build. It is ideal for photographers and divers who want a high-visibility experience with enough structure to explore.

Depth Range

Visibility

Recommended experience
Advanced open-water divers or guided snorkelers
