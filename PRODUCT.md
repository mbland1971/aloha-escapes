# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Affluent couples, honeymooners, and seasoned luxury travelers (35–60) planning a premium Hawaiian vacation. They travel 2–3 times a year, have discretionary budgets above $5,000 per trip, and expect the level of curation and personal attention they get from a private travel concierge. They arrive knowing Hawaii exists; they come to Aloha Escapes because they want the right version of it.

## Product Purpose

Aloha Escapes is a boutique luxury travel agency specializing exclusively in Hawaiian vacations. We book private villa stays, helicopter tours, guided cultural experiences, and curated inter-island itineraries — the things a search engine can't assemble. Success means a visitor calling or emailing us rather than booking a package on Expedia.

## Brand Personality

Intimate. Elemental. Unhurried.

The brand does not shout about value or savings. It conveys deep knowledge of the islands and genuine affection for them. A visitor should feel like they're receiving a private recommendation from a friend who lives there.

## Anti-references

- Generic resort aggregator sites (Expedia, Booking.com) — transactional, comparison-grid-heavy
- Chain hotel marketing — anonymous, benefit-bullet-list approach
- Budget travel blogs — low production value, cramped layouts, stock photo overload
- Anything with "world-class" / "unforgettable" / "paradise awaits" copy

## Design Principles

1. **Imagery leads, copy supports.** The islands speak for themselves; words frame what the photos already communicate.
2. **Specificity over superlatives.** Name the beach, the crater, the dive site. Avoid category adjectives like "stunning" and "breathtaking."
3. **Unhurried pacing.** Generous whitespace, deliberate scroll rhythm. Luxury is never rushed.
4. **One decision per section.** Each part of the page asks the visitor to feel one thing or consider one choice. No content collisions.
5. **Earned exclusivity.** Premium feel through restraint, craft, and specificity — not price tags, ratings badges, or social proof widgets.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. All images require descriptive alt text that names the specific place and scene. Motion respects prefers-reduced-motion. Sufficient contrast on all body text (≥4.5:1) and large text (≥3:1). Keyboard-navigable booking CTA.
